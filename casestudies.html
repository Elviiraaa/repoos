Småtrollen

Heuristic evaluation - Idea generating - Prototyping

Overview

Project

In this project a parent cooperative called Småtrollens föräldrarkooperativ wanted a new design for their website. They wanted a website that reflected them as organisation, which was warmth, playfulness and their enviornmental focus. In this project we kept on working agile in our team.

Goal

The goal of designing a new website for Småtrollen was to attract additional families to the cooperative. Since the children enrolled in the cooperative were mainly siblings and getting older, the need for more families arose.

Team

Marie Hagsjö, Peter Haraldsson, Catrin Johnsson

Scope

2 week sprint

Heuristic evaulation

A heuristic evaluation is a usability evaluation method used to identify usability issues in a user interface design. It involves having usability experts or evaluators review a user interface based on a set of predefined heuristics or guidelines, such as visibility of system status, user control and freedom, and error prevention.We chose four different heuristics that we felt matched with the clients needs, theese heuristics we then evaluated. This helped us identify the websites flaws and brought us ideas on what improvments we wanted to implement in our design. Our Heuristic findings

Designing User Interface

Brainstorming

We were given material from Småtrollens föräldrarkooperativ, such as information text, pictures, fonts and colors that they liked. They also informed us about their organization and what feeling they wanted to be delivered through the website. With this information we then started brainstorming by doing low-fidelity wireframes with pen and paper. With all of our different ideas we created a mindmap with all the content that the website was going to contain. We created the structure of the website, based on the heuristics we had chosen and gestalt principles.

Wireframing

We created mid-fidelity wireframes to gain a clearer vision of how to construct the website, which also saved us time as it is simpler to implement changes in a wireframe than in a prototype.

UX-copy

From our heursitic evaluation we found their text on the existing website to be reiterative and too long. As a result, we chose to rewrite and shorten down the information text.

Colors and fonts

We selected the colors that Småtrollen had wished for. They embody their organization's values of warmth, playfulness, and a connection to nature. The fontstyles we chose are well-known and easy-to-read fonts that have stood the test of time and are beloved.

Prototyping

We created a libary with components, fonts and colorstyles and started to creat our high-fidelity prototype. Since we had our mid-fidelity wireframes it made it easy for us to change the wireframes into prototypes.

In Progress

Småtrollen selected our team's design and another team's design from our class to be implemented on their website. Currently, we are in the process of designing the final user interface for their website.

What I've learned

  • A heuristic evaluation can help to improve the overall user experience of a product, by identifying usability issues and addressing them.
  • Making fast decisions can be challenging. Working with a short amount of time forces you to make fast decisions, which is not always simple.
  • I gained experience in communicating with a client to understand their needs and preferences. This can helped me develop stronger client management skills.
